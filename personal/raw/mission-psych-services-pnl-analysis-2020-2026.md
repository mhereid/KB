## 2. Maternity-leave cycles

Baby 1 (10/7/2019) predates the business, so only **two** maternity cycles actually appear inside the P&L. Each shows up as a distinct dip-and-recovery, and each recovery lands at a higher steady state than the prior cycle — a positive-feedback growth pattern for a referral-based solo practice.

### Practice launch — Feb 2020
- Feb 2020 first revenue: $185. Ramp: $300 (Apr) → $4,695 (May) → $8,199 (Jun) → $1,599 (Jul) → **$17,079 in Aug 2020** (likely an accumulated-claims catch-up, not a "normal" month — flag as an anomaly).
- Reached stable ~$7.5–8.5K/mo by Oct 2020 (~8 months from launch to steady state).

### Cycle 1 — Baby 2 (7/21/2021)
- Pre-leave slowdown visible in **May 2021 ($5,273)** — likely tapering intake.
- **Aug/Sep/Oct 2021: $0 revenue** (full maternity blackout).
- Nov 2021: $6,111 (return).
- Back to ~$8–9K/mo by Feb 2022 (~4 months to steady state — faster than the initial launch because the book of business already existed).

### Cycle 2 — Baby 3 (12/20/2023)
- **Jan/Feb/Mar 2024 near-zero**: $652, $360, $500 (trailing claims only).
- Apr 2024: $6,368 (return).
- Back to ~$9–11K by Sep–Oct 2024 (~7 months to a *higher* steady state).
- By Oct 2024, billing ($11,336) exceeded the pre-leave baseline.

**Insight:** Both in-business maternity cycles followed the same pattern — a ~3 month near-zero window, then a rapid return to a higher baseline than pre-leave. The practice is compounding through each cycle, not just recovering.

## 3. Revenue trajectory and the Mentalyc step-change

Rolling steady-state baselines (excluding leave months):
- 2020 H2 steady: ~$8.0K/mo
- 2022 steady: ~$8.0K/mo
- 2023 steady: ~$8.7K/mo
- 2024 H2 steady: ~$8.8K/mo
- **2025 steady: ~$10.1K/mo**
- 2026 YTD steady: ~$11.9K/mo

At a $180 session rate, $11.9K/mo ≈ 66 sessions/mo ≈ ~16/week — a realistic solo-practice load.

**Causal anchor:** The practice adopted **Mentalyc (AI note companion) in August 2024**. The revenue step-up begins the very next quarter:

| Period | Avg monthly revenue |
|---|---:|
| Apr–Jul 2024 (post-leave, pre-Mentalyc) | ~$7.9K |
| Aug–Dec 2024 (Mentalyc ramp) | ~$8.8K |
| Jan–Dec 2025 (first full Mentalyc year) | ~$10.1K |
| Jan–Mar 2026 | ~$11.9K |

Consistent with Mentalyc eliminating most post-session documentation time (a reported 30–60 min/day for solo therapists), which translates directly into more billable sessions per week without changing hours worked. The step-up is most plausibly a **capacity expansion**, not primarily a fee hike. December 2025 at **$13,564** and March 2026 at **$13,152** are all-time highs and sit squarely inside the Mentalyc era.

**Annualized run-rate for 2026** using Q1 actuals ($35,601 over 3 full months) ≈ **$142K revenue**. If March's level holds, full-year 2026 could reach **$155–160K**, a new record.

### Expense observations
1. **Owner payroll dominates** (66% of expenses). Monthly payroll has stabilized around $3,146–$3,158 since mid-2024, with a Dec year-end true-up of $13,406 in Dec 2025 (pushed Dec 2025 into a small net loss).
2. **Subscription growth is partly Mentalyc.** Dues & Subscriptions jumped from ~$30/mo (2022) to ~$200–280/mo steady in 2024–25 with periodic $500–900 spikes. A meaningful share of the 2024-onward increase is the Mentalyc subscription — high-ROI, not creep. Still worth a 15-minute audit of the other line items.
3. **Office Supplies & Software is lumpy** — $1,099 in Feb 2026 stands out against the ~$250/mo baseline. If annualized software, consider reclassing.
4. **Interest Paid is effectively zero after mid-2024** — prior loan appears paid off.
5. **Training is spiky and cyclical** — $700 Nov 2025, $1,074 Mar 2025, $555 Jul 2024. Typical CE requirements.
6. **Insurance** is annual-premium style (~$918–$1,041 once per year, mid-year).

## 7. Red flags & things to verify

1. **Aug 2020 $17,079 spike** — 2× the steady state. Almost certainly an insurance payer catch-up of backlogged claims, not a "normal" month. Don't anchor forecasts to it.
2. **Negative payroll-tax line items** (e.g., –$3,560 Jul 2023, –$3,600 Dec 2024, –$2,098 Jan 2024) are Q4/year-end true-ups or misclassed reversals — confirm with CPA they net correctly on the 941/DE-9.
3. **Dec 2025 $1,299 net loss** is entirely driven by the $13,406 owner-payroll bonus. Not operational — discretionary year-end distribution.
4. **Dues & Subscriptions drift** — up ~8× from 2022 baseline; 15-minute audit recommended.
5. **Apr 2024 ($6,368)** is unusually soft for a "return from leave" — worth comparing to session counts to verify full rebooking happened.

## 8. Key takeaways

1. **The business is compounding.** Post-launch baseline ~$8K/mo; post-Baby-2 ~$8.5K/mo; post-Baby-3 ~$12K/mo. 2025 set records on both revenue ($121K) and net income ($42K).
2. **2026 is pacing to a new all-time record** — Q1 annualized ≈ $142K revenue. If March's $13K/mo holds, full-year could touch $155–160K.
3. **The late-2024 capacity step-up is explained: Mentalyc.** Adopting the AI note companion in Aug 2024 directly precedes the jump from ~$8.5K/mo to ~$10–12K/mo. A ~$40–80/mo subscription that plausibly unlocked $20–30K/yr of additional revenue by freeing documentation time for billable sessions. Highest-ROI operational decision visible in the P&L.
4. **Summer (esp. July) is reliably ~20% soft.** Plan cash accordingly.
5. **Expense discipline is good overall** — 37% op margin is strong for a solo psych practice. Main opportunity is a subscriptions audit and reclassifying any annual software out of monthly Office Supplies.
6. **Maternity re-initiation is predictable** — ~3 month zero-revenue window followed by a 4–7 month ramp to a higher baseline; the practice's referral pipeline is self-sustaining.
