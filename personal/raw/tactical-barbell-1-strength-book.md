**Captured:** 2026-04-08

## Overview

Tactical Barbell I (TB1) is the maximal-strength training book in the TB system. It provides a template-based strength program designed for tactical/operational athletes who need to develop high levels of strength without sacrificing conditioning or other athletic attributes. The core philosophy: strength is a skill that should be practiced frequently with submaximal loads, avoiding failure, and using calculated progression.

## Core Principles

1. **Results over methods** — TB values measured outcomes, not trendy approaches
2. **Simplicity** — compound barbell lifts only, no fluff
3. **Strength as skill** — practice heavy lifting frequently while avoiding failure/overtraining
4. **Attributes vs Tools** — train the attribute (maximal-strength) using the best tool (barbell), don't confuse the tool for the goal
5. **Operational mindset** — strength must coexist with conditioning, not dominate it

## Key Concepts

### Strength vs Hypertrophy
- Myofibrillar development (1-6 reps, heavy loads) = force generation = TB1's focus
- Sarcoplasmic hypertrophy (8-12 reps, moderate loads) = muscle size = bodybuilding territory
- They overlap but optimize differently; TB1 prioritizes strength with hypertrophy as a side effect

### The Golden Rule
REST A MINIMUM OF TWO MINUTES BETWEEN SETS. Avoid muscle failure. You're practicing strength, not working out. For those wanting to minimize hypertrophy further, extend to 4-5 minute rest intervals. This is non-negotiable for the TB1 templates.

### Periodization Model
- Load percentages cycle from 70-95% of 1RM over 6-week blocks
- Intensity waves week to week (not linear daily increases)
- Stay with the same weight for multiple sessions before progressing
- Retest or force progression every 6-12 weeks
- Take deload/bridge weeks every 3-6 months

## The Three Templates

### Operator (3 days/week)
The flagship TB template. All exercises in the cluster performed every session, 3x/week (Days 1, 3, 5). Days 2, 4, 6 for conditioning. Day 7 rest.

**Operator Standard Template (6-week block):**

| Week | Sets × Reps | % 1RM |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | 3-5 × 5 | 70% |
| 2 | 3-5 × 5 | 80% |
| 3 | 3-4 × 3 | 90% |
| 4 | 3-5 × 5 | 75% |
| 5 | 3-5 × 3 | 85% |
| 6 | 3-4 × 1-2 | 95% |

All exercises performed every session at the same sets/reps/%. Example week:
- Day 1: SQ/BP/WPU @ prescribed %
- Day 3: SQ/BP/WPU @ prescribed %
- Day 5: SQ/BP/DL @ prescribed % (DL replaces WPU on 3rd session)

**Operator I/A Template (6-week block):**
Same percentages as Standard but with 3-5 set range for flexibility. Allows floating schedule (e.g., every 3rd day) instead of fixed 3x/week. For intermediate/advanced lifters whose recovery needs have changed at higher strength levels.

**Recommended Operator Clusters:**
- SWAT/HRT/generalist: Squat / Bench Press / Weighted Pull-up + optional DL
- SOF/infantry/endurance: Front Squat / OHP / Weighted Pull-up + optional DL
- Strength-priority: Squat / Bench Press / Deadlift (DL every session, 1 work set)

### Zulu (4 days/week)
Max-flex template. Cluster split into A/B groups, each performed 2x/week. Brief sessions (~20-30 min), time for conditioning or accessories after.

**Zulu Standard Template (6-week block):**

| Week | Sets × Reps | % 1RM |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | 3 × 5 | 70% |
| 2 | 3 × 5 | 80% |
| 3 | 3 × 3 | 90% |
| 4 | 3 × 5 | 70% |
| 5 | 3 × 5 | 80% |
| 6 | 3 × 3 | 90% |

Schedule: Day 1 = A, Day 2 = B, Day 3 = off, Day 4 = A, Day 5 = B, Days 6-7 = off

**Zulu I/A Template (6-week block):**

| Week | Sets × Reps | % 1RM |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | 3-5 × 5 | 70% |
| 2 | 3-5 × 5 | 80% |
| 3 | 3-5 × 3 | 90% |
| 4 | 3-5 × 5 | 75% |
| 5 | 3-5 × 3 | 85% |
| 6 | 3-5 × 1-2 | 95% |

Flexible volume (3-5 sets) per exercise based on recovery, conditioning load, and goals. Same A/B/off/A/B/off schedule.

**Recommended Zulu Clusters:**
- BP / SQ / OHP / DL (divide: A = BP/SQ, B = OHP/DL)
- BP / SQ / OHP / DL / WPU (divide: A = SQ/OHP/WPU, B = DL/BP)
- BP / SQ / OHP / DL / Power Clean
- Minimalist: BP / DL only (each performed 2x/week, ~15 min sessions)

### Fighter (2 days/week)
Minimalist template for athletes with heavy conditioning/skills loads. Lowest effective dose.

**Fighter Standard Template (6-week block):**

| Week | Sets × Reps | % 1RM |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | 3-5 × 5 | 75% |
| 2 | 3-5 × 5 | 80% |
| 3 | 3-5 × 3 | 90% |
| 4 | 3-5 × 5 | 75% |
| 5 | 3-5 × 5 | 80% |
| 6 | 3-5 × 3 | 90% |

Schedule: Day 1 = session 1, Day 4 = session 2, all other days for conditioning/sport/rest. Each lift performed 2x/week across the 2 sessions.

Example: Day 1: BP/SQ/WPU, Day 4: BP/SQ + DL × 1-3 work sets

**Fighter I/A ("Bangkok version"):** Adds progressive SE training alongside maximal-strength. For those requiring year-round strength-endurance.

**Recommended Fighter Clusters:**
- BP / SQ / WPU + optional DL
- SQ / DL (minimalist, popular with marathon runners)
- BP / SQ / DL

## Exercise Clusters

### Cluster Sizes
- **Minimalist** (2-3 exercises): For heavy conditioning loads or low-priority strength. Example: Squat + Deadlift for a marathon runner.
- **Standard** (3-4 exercises): Best balance of strength and conditioning. Most effective for body composition. Example: Bench/Squat/Weighted Pull-up + DL.
- **Heavy** (4+ exercises): Used primarily with Zulu or Fighter. For those with more time/energy for strength. Example: Bench/Squat/DL/OHP/WPU/Rows.

### Key Cluster Principles
- Balance can come from activities outside the strength protocol (conditioning, sport, skills)
- Compound lifts only for main cluster; accessories are bonus, not required
- Getting very strong in 3 core lifts will increase overall strength across all movements
- Don't change clusters mid-block; consistency drives results

## Incorporating Deadlifts
Multiple options for managing deadlift volume/frequency:
- 1x/week: 1-3 work sets on the third strength session, replacing WPU
- 3x/week: 1 work set each session added after main cluster
- The finicky nature of deadlifts is acknowledged; some thrive on volume, others need minimal exposure

## Forced Progression
Alternative to retesting: add 5-10 lbs to 1RMs every 6-12 weeks and recalculate all percentages. No retesting required. Decision tree: if all lifts felt manageable, add weight. If you struggled, keep same numbers. Can progress individual lifts independently.

## Strength-Endurance (SE)
- TB1 includes SE circuits as a complement to maximal-strength
- High-rep, light-load (15-30% 1RM) circuits performed as separate sessions
- Builds work capacity, connective tissue strength, and muscular endurance
- SE training is messy by nature — failure is expected and acceptable
- Introductory SE model included in this edition for beginners

## Nutrition (Brief)
- TB1 is not prescriptive about nutrition beyond general principles
- Eat to support training and goals; adjust for activity level
- For strength without excessive mass: control food intake, increase rest intervals
- For mass gain: eat surplus, use 2-min rest intervals, run Mass Protocol templates

## Supplements
- Level 1: Creatine monohydrate, protein powder, multivitamin
- Level 2: Caffeine, fish oil
- Same tier system as Mass Protocol
