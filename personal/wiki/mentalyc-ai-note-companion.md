# Mentalyc AI Note Companion

Mentalyc is an AI clinical documentation tool that auto-generates therapy progress notes from session audio, eliminating most of the post-session paperwork burden for mental-health providers. Stephanie's practice ([[mission-psychological-services]]) adopted Mentalyc in **August 2024**, and the P&L shows it as the single highest-ROI operational decision the business has made — a ~$40–80/mo subscription that plausibly unlocked $20–30K/yr of additional revenue by freeing documentation time for billable sessions. [source](../raw/mission-psych-services-pnl-analysis-2020-2026.md)

## Why It Matters for a Solo Practice

Solo therapists typically report 30–60 minutes per day of post-session documentation. That time is unbillable but mandatory for insurance, compliance, and continuity of care. An AI note companion that drafts progress notes from session audio converts that reclaimed time directly into **more billable session capacity** — without lengthening the workday.

For an insurance-billing practice at ~$180/session, one additional session per day, three days per week ≈ $28K/year in gross revenue at effectively zero marginal cost beyond the subscription. That math is why Mentalyc looks like the best dollar-for-dollar investment visible in the [[mission-psychological-services]] P&L. [source](../raw/mission-psych-services-pnl-analysis-2020-2026.md)

## Bookkeeping Note

Mentalyc's subscription cost is part of the rise in the **Dues & Subscriptions** expense line from ~$30/mo (2022) to ~$200–280/mo steady in 2024–25. What looks like "subscription creep" in the category is partly a single high-ROI tool paying for itself many times over. When auditing subscriptions for cuts, separate Mentalyc from the other line items before making decisions. [source](../raw/mission-psych-services-pnl-analysis-2020-2026.md)

## Generalizable Lesson

The broader pattern worth protecting and replicating: **tool-driven leverage that reclaims unbillable time**. For a solo service practice, the highest-ROI software is rarely the thing that makes billable work faster — it's the thing that shrinks the unbillable overhead around each billable unit. Other candidates to evaluate through the same lens: scheduling automation, insurance-claim submission tools, intake/telehealth consent workflows, and billing reconciliation.
