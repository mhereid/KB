# Mission Psychological Services

Mission Psychological Services, Inc. is Stephanie's solo-provider California S-corp psychology practice, founded February 2020. The P&L shows a business that has compounded cleanly through two in-business maternity cycles to reach a 2025 record year (~$121K revenue / ~$42K net income) and is pacing to a new all-time high in 2026. The dominant recent trend is a clear capacity step-up in late 2024 that traces directly to adopting an AI note companion ([[mentalyc-ai-note-companion|Mentalyc]]) in August 2024. ## Maternity-Leave Cycles as a Revenue Pattern

Baby 1 predates the business, so only two maternity cycles actually appear in the P&L. Both follow the same shape — a ~3 month near-zero window followed by a 4–7 month ramp back to a **higher** steady-state baseline than before leave:

- **Cycle 1 (Baby 2, 7/21/2021):** Aug/Sep/Oct 2021 = $0. Return Nov 2021 ($6,111). Stable ~$8–9K/mo by Feb 2022.
- **Cycle 2 (Baby 3, 12/20/2023):** Jan/Feb/Mar 2024 near-zero ($652, $360, $500). Return Apr 2024 ($6,368). Stable ~$9–11K/mo by Sep–Oct 2024, with Oct 2024 ($11,336) already *exceeding* the pre-leave baseline.

The practice is compounding through each cycle, not just recovering. The referral pipeline is now self-sustaining enough that each return lands at a higher equilibrium. [source](../raw/mission-psych-services-pnl-analysis-2020-2026.md)

## The Mentalyc Step-Up (Aug 2024)

The single most important recent trend in the P&L is a capacity expansion that traces to adopting [[mentalyc-ai-note-companion|Mentalyc]] in August 2024. Steady-state monthly revenue before and after:

| Period | Avg monthly revenue |
|---|---:|
| Apr–Jul 2024 (post-leave, pre-Mentalyc) | ~$7.9K |
| Aug–Dec 2024 (Mentalyc ramp) | ~$8.8K |
| Jan–Dec 2025 (first full Mentalyc year) | ~$10.1K |
| Jan–Mar 2026 | ~$11.9K |

At a $180/session rate, ~$11.9K/mo equals roughly 16 sessions/week — a realistic solo-provider load. Because session fees did not materially change, the step-up is best explained as **capacity expansion**, not a price hike: Mentalyc eliminated most post-session documentation time (reported 30–60 min/day of reclaim for solo therapists), which converts directly into more billable sessions per week without changing hours worked. A ~$40–80/mo subscription plausibly unlocked **$20–30K/yr** of additional revenue — the highest-ROI operational decision visible in the P&L. [source](../raw/mission-psych-services-pnl-analysis-2020-2026.md)

## Red Flags & Things to Verify

1. **Aug 2020 $17,079 revenue spike** — 2× steady state; almost certainly an insurance payer catch-up of backlogged claims. Not a normal month; don't anchor forecasts to it.
2. **Negative payroll-tax line items** (–$3,560 Jul 2023, –$3,600 Dec 2024, –$2,098 Jan 2024) are Q4/year-end true-ups or reversals — confirm with CPA they net correctly on the 941/DE-9.
3. **Dec 2025 $1,299 net loss** is entirely the $13,406 owner-payroll bonus — discretionary distribution, not operational.
4. **Dues & Subscriptions drift** — 15-minute audit recommended to confirm only high-ROI tools remain.
5. **Apr 2024 ($6,368)** is unusually soft for a "return from leave" month — worth comparing against session counts to verify full rebooking happened.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The business is compounding.** Post-launch baseline ~$8K/mo → post-Baby-2 ~$8.5K/mo → post-Baby-3 ~$12K/mo.
2. **2026 is pacing to a new all-time record.** Q1 annualized ≈ $142K revenue; if March's $13K/mo holds, full-year could touch $155–160K.
3. **Mentalyc is the highest-ROI decision in the P&L.** Protect this workflow; watch for similar tool-driven leverage in scheduling, intake, and billing automation.
4. **July is reliably ~20% soft** — plan cash accordingly.
5. **Maternity re-initiation is predictable** — ~3 month zero window, then 4–7 month ramp to a higher baseline.
