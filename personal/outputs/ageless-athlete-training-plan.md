## Situation Assessment

You're in the position Jim Madden describes when he returns from his summer endurance phase: aerobic base is full, but you're out of practice under the bar. The Ageless Athlete prescribes base building → Operator continuation with 3-1-3-1 recovery.

**The 30+ mpw wrinkle:** The Ageless Athlete caps LSS at 3 × 30 min/week as the "minimal effective dose." You're running roughly 3× that volume. Madden explicitly addresses this — he himself does far more endurance than the minimum because he enjoys it and has strength to spare, but warns it comes at some expense to other attributes. The trade-off is manageable if you:

1. Are **extra conservative** with training maxes (your legs are absorbing ~30 miles of impact per week on top of squats)
2. Treat running quality sessions as your HIC (they count — no need for separate conditioning)
3. Accept that strength gains will come **slower** than for someone doing minimal conditioning — and that's fine
4. Monitor recovery ruthlessly and drop mileage before dropping lifting when something has to give

Your running volume means you're effectively always in a Fighter/Operator hybrid mindset: strength is a necessary but carefully managed priority alongside a high endurance load. This is exactly the kind of athlete TB was designed for.

## Phase 1: Modified Ageless Athlete Base Building (Weeks 1-6)

### Why Not Skip This
After 4 months without lifting, jumping straight into Operator at any percentage risks:
- Connective tissue injury (tendons/ligaments haven't been loaded)
- CNS shock from barbell work
- DOMS so severe it craters your running for a week

The Tango circuits rebuild work capacity and general physical preparedness at low load. The book is emphatic: the real benefit isn't SE improvement — it's gently strengthening muscles and ligaments while everything recalibrates for barbell loading.

### Weeks 1-4: SE + Running

| Day | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday | Sunday |
|-----|--------|---------|-----------|----------|--------|----------|--------|
| **AM** | Tango Circuit | — | — | Tango Circuit | — | — | — |
| **Run** | Easy 5 mi | Quality Run (5-7 mi) | Easy 6 mi | Easy 5 mi | Off or Easy 4 mi | Long Run 8-10 mi | **Off** |
| **Weekly Mileage** | | | | | | | **~30-34 mi** |

**Tango Circuit Progression:**

| Week | Circuits | Protocol |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1 | 2 rounds | 1 min AMRAP per exercise, 1 min RI between exercises, 2-3 min RI between circuits |
| 2 | 3 rounds | Same rest intervals |
| 3 | 3 rounds | Compress rest intervals (30-45 sec between exercises) |
| 4 | 4 rounds | Compress rest intervals further |

**Recommended Tango Circuit (6 exercises):**
1. KB Swings (2-handed, moderate weight)
2. Push-ups
3. Goblet Squats (light-moderate KB)
4. Inverted Rows (or Ring Rows)
5. KB Romanian Deadlift (light)
6. Plank (hold for duration)

Covers all movement patterns (hinge, squat, push, pull, core) at low load. Expect DOMS from goblet squats and RDLs in Week 1 — this is normal and will be gone by Week 2. Schedule Tango on Monday/Thursday to keep max distance from Saturday long run.

### Weeks 5-6: Reintroduce Barbell

| Day | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday | Sunday |
|-----|--------|---------|-----------|----------|--------|----------|--------|
| **AM** | Max Strength | — | Max Strength | — | — | — | — |
| **Run** | Easy 5 mi (PM) | Quality Run (5-7 mi) | Easy 5 mi (PM) | Easy 6 mi | Off or Easy 4 mi | Long Run 8-10 mi | **Off** |
| **Weekly Mileage** | | | | | | | **~30-34 mi** |

**Max Strength Sessions (Weeks 5-6):**
- Cluster: **Back Squat / Bench Press / Weighted Pull-up**
- 3 × 5 at a weight that feels like RPE 6-7 (3-4 reps in the tank)
- Start with just the bar, work up methodically
- Rest 2-3 min between all sets (Golden Rule)
- Add 50-70 KB Swings after one session, Farmer Carries after the other
- Only 2 lifting days/week — your legs need to share the road with running

**By end of Week 6:** You should have a solid feel for your working weights. Use your best "chill" sets of 5 to establish training maxes (see below).

## Establishing Training Maxes

Set training maxes **extra conservatively** because of the running volume. Your legs are absorbing 30+ miles/week of impact, which means squat and deadlift recovery is compromised vs. a non-runner.

| Method | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| **Recommended:** | Use your best "chill" set of 5 from Weeks 5-6. That weight IS your training max. |
| **Alternative:** | Estimate 1RM from best set of 5, then take **85%** as training max (not the standard 90%). |

The Ageless Athlete is emphatic: put all 6 weeks in a spreadsheet. If ANY day looks questionable after factoring in the previous day's run, lower the training max. You should be able to hit every number on your worst day.

**Example (hypothetical starting point):**

| Lift | Best "Chill" 5 (Week 5-6) | Training Max | Week 1 (75%) | Week 2 (80%) | Week 3 (90%) |
|------|---------------------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Back Squat | 185 × 5 | 185 | 140 | 150 | 165 |
| Bench Press | 155 × 5 | 155 | 115 | 125 | 140 |
| Weighted Pull-up | BW+15 × 5 | BW+15 | BW+11 | BW+12 | BW+14 |

These will feel light. That's the point. You're rebuilding the skill of lifting while running 30+ mpw. The weights will climb with forced progressions.

## Phase 2 Continued: Block 2 (Weeks 15-22)

### Forced Progression Decision

At the end of Block 1, assess each lift independently:

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| All reps at 90% were crisp, bar moving fast | Add 10 lbs to squat/DL training max, 5 lbs to bench/WPU |
| Most reps good but a couple felt heavy | Add 5 lbs to squat/DL, keep bench/WPU the same |
| Struggled at 90% | Keep same training maxes — you need another block at this level |

**Important for runners:** The squat is most likely to stall first because your legs never fully recover between runs. Don't force squat progression if it wasn't crisp. Your bench and WPU will likely progress faster — let them.

Recalculate all percentages with new training maxes. Run another 3-1-3-1 block.

### Volume Adjustment for Block 2

Pull back slightly from Block 1:
- 75% weeks: 3-5 sets per lift
- 85% weeks: 3-4 sets
- 90% weeks: 3 sets

Same weekly template. Same running structure.

## HIC / Quality Run Warm-Up Protocol

1. Dynamic stretching: hamstrings, hips, calves, upper body (5 min)
2. 800m-1 mile easy jog
3. 2-3 strides/building sprints at ~80% effort
4. First 1-2 working reps are diagnostic — gauge what kind of day it is
5. Build intensity through the session
6. Any twinge → moderate or shut down

## Monitoring: When to Adjust

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Legs feel dead on Monday squat after Sunday off | Lower squat training max OR shorten Saturday long run |
| Can't hit prescribed reps at 85-90% | Was Tuesday's quality run too hard? Swap in RowErg next week |
| Run paces slowing despite easy effort | Lifting volume too high — drop to minimum sets (3 per lift) |
| Persistent soreness lasting >48 hours | Take a variable rest day, drop one run that week |
| Everything feels great at 90% | You set your training max correctly. Force progression next block. |
| Friday deadlift tanking your Saturday long run | Move DL to Wednesday, do WPU on Friday instead |

The Ageless Athlete's core message: **be your own coach.** The templates are guardrails, not commandments. When running and lifting compete for recovery, you need to make real-time decisions. The moderation principle applies — neither attribute gets to dominate at the expense of the other.

## Key Principles to Remember

1. **Training maxes are sacred.** Hit every number on your worst day — even the day after a hard tempo run.
2. **The Golden Rule is non-negotiable.** Minimum 2-minute rest between all sets. You're practicing strength, not working out.
3. **Running and lifting intensity are inversely correlated.** When the bar gets heavy, the runs get easy. When the bar is light, push the roads.
4. **The RowErg is your escape valve.** When legs are too beat for a quality run, swap in RowErg intervals — same conditioning effect, zero impact.
5. **Don't force squat progression.** Your legs are running 30+ mpw. Squat gains will come slower than bench/WPU. That's the trade-off, and it's worth it.
6. **Discipline over intensity.** Show up, do the minimums, go home. The days you don't feel like training are the most important days to show up.
7. **Think in decades.** You're 39. The question isn't where you'll be in 12 weeks — it's where you'll be at 49.
8. **Preemptive recovery.** The 3-1-3-1 isn't a concession — it's an active strategy.
9. **Don't go low-carb.** You're running 30+ mpw and lifting 3x/week. Glycogen fuels both. Eat the carbs.
