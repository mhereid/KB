**Weekly running mileage:** 5-6 + 6-7 + 6 + 10-12 = **~27-31 mi**

### Why This Sequence Works

- **Tuesday** is the big double day (~1:45) but you're coming off Monday rest with fresh legs. Lift first while CNS is sharpest, then easy run. The run is at easy pace — treat it as an extended cooldown.
- **Wednesday** quality run is 24 hrs after Tuesday's squat and 24 hrs before Thursday's squat. This is the furthest possible from Saturday's heavy deadlift day.
- **Thursday** HIC is RowErg intervals tacked onto lifting. Rower is concentric-dominant and zero-impact — fine after squats. Total session ~75 min.
- **Friday** is pure easy running. Legs recover from Thursday's double and prep for Saturday's heaviest lifting day (squat + deadlift).
- **Saturday** is lifting only — no running. The heaviest barbell session of the week gets maximum focus. Done by 6 AM, then full recovery through the day.
- **Sunday** long run at easy pace. Saturday DL → Sunday long run works because long runs are conversational effort and the 24 hr gap is sufficient.
- **Monday** full off after Sunday's long run. Complete system reset.

## Quality Run Menu (Wednesday — pick 1)

| Session | Protocol | Miles |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Tempo Run** | 1.5 mi easy + 3-4 mi @ marathon pace or faster + 1 mi easy | 6-7 |
| **800m Repeats** | 1.5 mi warm-up + 4-6 × 800m @ 5K pace (400m jog recovery) + 1 mi cool-down | 5-7 |
| **Hill Repeats** | 1.5 mi warm-up + 8-12 × 60-90 sec uphill (jog down) + 1 mi cool-down | 5-6 |
| **Progression Run** | Start easy, drop 15-20 sec/mi each mile for last 3-4 miles | 7-8 |
| **Fun Run** | 6-8 mi easy + calisthenics every 6 min (10 push-ups + 10 squats) | 6-8 |
| **Apex Hill Sprints** | Sprint up + 10 × 2H KB swings + jog down × 8-15 | ~2-3 equiv |

Use Apex as an occasional swap (1-2x per block) — it's a TB classic but hits legs harder than other options. Standard rotation: alternate between tempo runs and 800m repeats, with hill repeats and progression runs mixed in for variety.

## RowErg HIC Menu (Thursday — pick 1)

| Session | Protocol | Duration |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **500m Repeats** | 6-8 × 500m, 2-3 min rest | ~20 min |
| **Power Intervals** | 5-7 × 30 sec max effort, 3 min rest | ~18 min |
| **2K Intervals** | 3 × 2000m, 3-4 min rest | ~22 min |
| **Tabata Rows** | 8 × 20 sec max / 10 sec rest, 2 min rest, repeat 2-3 rounds | ~12-15 min |
| **Fobbits (Rower)** | 15-20 min steady rowing, 10 KB swings every 2 min | 15-20 min |

**Default:** 500m repeats. Simple, scalable, and easy to track progress (PR your split times). Fobbits is the low-intensity option for weeks when everything hurts.

## Phase 1: Modified Ageless Athlete Base Building (Weeks 1-6)

### Why Not Skip This
After 4 months without lifting, jumping straight into Operator risks connective tissue injury, CNS shock, and DOMS severe enough to crater your running for a week. The Tango circuits rebuild work capacity at low load. The book is emphatic: the real benefit is gently strengthening muscles and ligaments while everything recalibrates.

### Weeks 1-4: SE + Running

| Day | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun | Mon |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| **5 AM** | Tango Circuit + Easy 5 mi | Workout Run 6-7 mi | Tango Circuit + RowErg HIC | Easy 6 mi | Off or Easy 4 mi | Long 8-10 mi | **OFF** |
| **Duration** | ~1:30 | ~1:00 | ~1:00 | ~0:55 | ~0:35 | ~1:20-1:40 | — |

**Tango Circuit Progression:**

| Week | Circuits | Protocol |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1 | 2 rounds | 1 min AMRAP per exercise, 1 min RI between exercises, 2-3 min RI between circuits |
| 2 | 3 rounds | Same rest intervals |
| 3 | 3 rounds | Compress RI (30-45 sec between exercises) |
| 4 | 4 rounds | Compress RI further |

**Recommended Tango Circuit (6 exercises):**
1. KB Swings (2-handed, moderate weight)
2. Push-ups
3. Goblet Squats (light-moderate KB)
4. Inverted Rows (or Ring Rows)
5. KB Romanian Deadlift (light)
6. Plank (hold for duration)

Saturday during base building is optional — an easy 4 mi shakeout, or take it off entirely if legs are sore from Tango circuit goblet squats (they will be in Week 1).

### Weeks 5-6: Reintroduce Barbell

| Day | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun | Mon |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| **5 AM** | Operator (light) + Easy 5 mi | Workout Run 6-7 mi | Operator (light) + RowErg HIC | Easy 6 mi | Off | Long 8-10 mi | **OFF** |

- Cluster: **Back Squat / Bench Press / Weighted Pull-up**
- 3 × 5 at RPE 6-7 (3-4 reps in the tank)
- Start with just the bar, work up methodically
- Rest 2-3 min between all sets (Golden Rule)
- Only 2 lifting days — no Saturday session yet. Connective tissue needs gradual loading.

**By end of Week 6:** Use your best "chill" sets of 5 to establish training maxes.

## Establishing Training Maxes

Set training maxes **extra conservatively** — your legs are absorbing 30 mpw of impact on top of squats.

| Method | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| **Recommended** | Best "chill" set of 5 from Weeks 5-6 = your training max |
| **Alternative** | Estimate 1RM from best set of 5, take **85%** (not standard 90%) |

Put all weeks in a spreadsheet. If ANY day looks questionable after factoring in the previous day's run, lower the training max. You should be able to hit every number on your worst day.

**Example (hypothetical):**

| Lift | Best "Chill" 5 | Training Max | 75% | 80% | 85% | 90% |
|------|----------------|-------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Back Squat | 185 × 5 | 185 | 140 | 150 | 157 | 165 |
| Bench Press | 155 × 5 | 155 | 116 | 124 | 132 | 140 |
| Weighted Pull-up | BW+15 × 5 | BW+15 | BW+11 | BW+12 | BW+13 | BW+14 |

## Phase 2 Continued: Block 2 (Weeks 15-22)

### Forced Progression Decision

Assess each lift independently after Block 1:

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| All reps at 90% crisp, bar moving fast | +10 lbs squat/DL training max, +5 lbs bench/WPU |
| Most reps good, a couple felt heavy | +5 lbs squat/DL, keep bench/WPU same |
| Struggled at 90% | Keep same training maxes — run another block |

**For runners:** Squat is most likely to stall first (legs never fully recover between runs). Don't force squat progression if it wasn't crisp. Bench and WPU will likely progress faster — let them.

### Block 2 Volume

Pull back slightly:
- 75% weeks: 3-5 sets per lift
- 85% weeks: 3-4 sets
- 90% weeks: 3 sets

Same weekly template. Same running structure. Same easy week / recovery week rules.

## Monitoring: When to Adjust

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Legs feel dead on Tuesday squat after Monday off | Lower squat training max — your long run is too much to recover from |
| Can't hit prescribed reps at 85-90% | Was Wednesday's quality run too hard? Swap to progression run or easy run |
| Run paces slowing despite easy effort | Lifting volume too high — drop to minimum sets (3 per lift) |
| Persistent soreness lasting >48 hours | Drop one run that week (cut Tuesday to lift-only) |
| Everything feels great at 90% | Training max is correct. Force progression next block. |
| Saturday deadlift tanking Sunday long run | Drop DL to 1 work set, or move DL to Thursday and skip RowErg that day |
| Tuesday double too long at 5 AM | Drop the run to 4 mi, or skip it and add miles to Friday (make it 7 mi) |
| Thursday RowErg feels pointless/easy | Progress: increase intervals (6→8 × 500m), shorten rest, or add 2K intervals |

## Key Principles

1. **Training maxes are sacred.** Hit every number on your worst day — even after a hard tempo run the day before.
2. **The Golden Rule is non-negotiable.** Minimum 2-minute rest between all sets.
3. **Running and lifting intensity are inversely correlated.** When the bar gets heavy, the runs get easy. When the bar is light, push the roads.
4. **The RowErg is your escape valve.** Zero-impact conditioning when legs are beat. Default HIC for a runner-lifter.
5. **Don't force squat progression.** Legs are running 30 mpw. Squat gains come slower than bench/WPU. That's the trade-off.
6. **Tuesday is the big day.** Fresh off Monday rest = highest capacity for a double. If it starts feeling unsustainable, cut the run first, never the lift.
7. **Saturday is sacred lift-only.** The heaviest session gets maximum focus. No running.
8. **Think in decades.** You're 39. Where will you be at 49? Patience and consistency beat intensity every time.
9. **Don't go low-carb.** Glycogen fuels both the roads and the bar.
