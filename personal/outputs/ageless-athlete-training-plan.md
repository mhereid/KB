## Situation Assessment

You're in almost exactly the position Jim Madden describes when he returns from his summer endurance phase: aerobic base is full, but you're "a little skinny and out of practice" for barbell work. The Ageless Athlete prescribes a specific sequence for this:

1. **Base Building** to prep connective tissue and maintain aerobic base
2. **Continuation** with conservative training maxes, high volume, progressive intensity
3. **3-1-3-1 recovery** cadence throughout

The key difference from standard TB: your aerobic tank is *already full* from marathon training. You don't need 5 weeks of LSS to build a base — you need the SE circuits to re-condition muscles, ligaments, and joints for barbell work, plus maintained (not built) endurance.

## Phase 1: Modified Ageless Athlete Base Building (Weeks 1-6)

### Why Not Skip This
After 4 months without lifting, jumping straight into Operator at any percentage risks:
- Connective tissue injury (tendons/ligaments haven't been loaded)
- CNS shock from barbell work
- DOMS so severe it derails Week 2

The Tango circuits rebuild work capacity and general physical preparedness. The book is emphatic: the real benefit isn't SE improvement — it's gently strengthening muscles and ligaments while letting everything recalibrate.

### Weeks 1-4: SE + Maintained Aerobic Base

| Day | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday | Sunday |
|-----|--------|---------|-----------|----------|--------|----------|--------|
| **Activity** | Tango Circuit | LSS 30 min | LSS 30 min | Tango Circuit | Recovery / Mobility | LSS 30 min | **Off** |

**Tango Circuit Progression:**

| Week | Circuits | Protocol |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1 | 2 rounds | 1 min AMRAP per exercise, 1 min RI between exercises, 2-3 min RI between circuits |
| 2 | 3 rounds | Same rest intervals |
| 3 | 3 rounds | Compress rest intervals (reduce RI between exercises to 30-45 sec) |
| 4 | 4 rounds | Compress rest intervals further |

**Recommended Tango Circuit (6 exercises):**
1. KB Swings (2-handed, moderate weight)
2. Push-ups
3. Goblet Squats (light-moderate KB)
4. Inverted Rows (or Ring Rows)
5. KB Romanian Deadlift (light)
6. Plank (hold for duration)

This circuit covers all major patterns (hinge, squat, push, pull, core) at low load to prep everything for barbell work. Swap exercises as needed — the point is bodyweight + light KB, high reps, building work capacity.

**LSS Sessions:** Easy running, cycling, or swimming at conversational pace. You're maintaining your marathon base, not building it. 30 minutes max. Observe the talk test strictly. This is also your post-marathon recovery — don't race these.

### Weeks 5-6: Reintroduce Barbell + HIC

| Day | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday | Sunday |
|-----|--------|---------|-----------|----------|--------|----------|--------|
| **Activity** | Max Strength | HIC | Recovery / Mobility | Max Strength | HIC | LSS 30 min | **Off** |

**Max Strength Sessions (Weeks 5-6):**
- Use this to find your working weights — you're feeling things out, not training heavy
- Start with just the bar and work up methodically
- Cluster: **Back Squat / Bench Press / Weighted Pull-up**
- 3 × 5 at a weight that feels like a 6-7 RPE (leaves 3-4 reps in the tank)
- Rest 2-3 min between all sets (Golden Rule)
- Add KB Swings (50-70 total) after lifting on one day, Farmer Carries on the other

**HIC Options (pick 1 per session):**
- Apex Hill Sprints (start with 5-8 rounds, light KB — 24kg or less)
- 600m Resets × 4-5
- Airdyne Power Intervals × 5-6 (3 min rest between)

**By end of Week 6:** You should have a solid feel for your current working weights on SQ/BP/WPU. Use your best sets of 5 from these two weeks to establish training maxes (see below).

## Establishing Training Maxes

After Weeks 5-6, you'll have working weights for each lift. Set training maxes conservatively:

| Method | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| **Option A (recommended):** | Use your best "chill" set of 5 from base building. That weight IS your training max. |
| **Option B:** | Estimate 1RM from best set of 5, then take 90% as training max. |

The Ageless Athlete is emphatic: put all 6 weeks in a spreadsheet. If ANY day looks questionable, lower the training max. You should be able to hit every number on your worst day — head cold, bad sleep, sore from yesterday's conditioning.

**Example (hypothetical starting point):**

| Lift | Best "Chill" 5 (Week 5-6) | Training Max | Week 1 (70%) | Week 2 (80%) | Week 3 (90%) |
|------|---------------------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Back Squat | 185 × 5 | 185 | 130 | 150 | 165 |
| Bench Press | 155 × 5 | 155 | 110 | 125 | 140 |
| Weighted Pull-up | BW+15 × 5 | BW+15 | BW+10 | BW+12 | BW+14 |

These will feel light. That's the point. You're rebuilding the skill of lifting, greasing the groove, and letting connective tissue adapt. The weights will climb fast with forced progressions.

### Block 1 Wave 2 (Weeks 11-13)

Same structure as Wave 1 but with Operator I/A percentages (75→85→90%):

| Week | Sets × Reps | % 1RM | Volume | Conditioning |
|------|-------------|-------|--------|-------------|

During the 90% week (Week 13), everything should still feel crisp and snappy — if you set your training max correctly, these weights are well within your capacity. If the bar is moving fast at 90%, you're ready for a forced progression.

### Week 14: Recovery Week

Same as Week 10. No barbells, no HIC, max 2 easy sessions + mobility.

## Phase 2 Continued: Block 2 (Weeks 15-22)

### Forced Progression Decision

At the end of Block 1, assess each lift independently:

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| All reps at 90% were crisp, bar moving fast | Add 10 lbs to squat/DL training max, 5 lbs to bench/WPU |
| Most reps good but a couple felt heavy | Add 5 lbs to squat/DL, keep bench/WPU the same |
| Struggled at 90% | Keep same training maxes — you need another block at this level |

Recalculate all percentages with new training maxes. Run another 3-1-3-1 block (Weeks 15-17 training, Week 18 recovery, Weeks 19-21 training, Week 22 recovery).

### Volume Adjustment for Block 2

Pull back slightly from Block 1:
- 75% weeks: 4-6 sets (vs 5-7)
- 80-85% weeks: 3-5 sets
- 90% weeks: 3-4 sets

You can start transitioning from Operator I/A to Standard Operator sets/reps if preferred.

## Conditioning Plan

### HIC Menu (pick 1-2 per week)

| Session | Protocol | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Apex Hill Sprints** | Sprint up + 10 × 2H KB swings + jog down | Start at 5-8 rounds, progress to 12-15. Use 24kg KB initially. 1x/week max for aging athletes |
| **600m Resets** | 600m run at ~80% effort, 2-3 min rest, repeat | Start at 4 rounds, build to 6 |
| **Airdyne Power Intervals** | 20-30 sec max effort, 3 min rest | Start at 5 rounds, build to 7 |
| **Speed Endurance Ladder** | 200m-400m-600m-400m-200m with equal rest | 1-2 ladders |
| **Hill Sprints (plain)** | Sprint up 100-200m, jog down, repeat | 8-12 rounds, no KB |

### LSS Options (1-2 per week, 30 min max)

- Easy run at 9+ min/mile (talk test — you should be able to hold a conversation)
- Cycling 30 min at easy pace
- Swimming 20-30 min
- Fun Run with light calisthenics every 6 min (Standard Issue style)

### HIC Warm-Up Protocol (Every Session)

1. Dynamic stretching: hamstrings, hips, calves, upper body (5 min)
2. 800m jog or 5 min easy effort
3. 2-3 building sprints at ~80% effort
4. First 1-2 working reps are diagnostic — gauge what kind of day it is
5. Build intensity through the session
6. Any twinge → moderate or shut down

### Conditioning Volume by Lifting Week

| Lifting Week Intensity | Max Conditioning |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| 70-75% (light) | 2-3 sessions (1-2 HIC + 1 LSS) |
| 80-85% (moderate) | 2 sessions (1 HIC + 1 LSS) |
| 90% (heavy) | 1 HIC (reduced) + 1 easy LSS |
| Recovery week | 1-2 easy LSS only, no HIC |

## Key Principles to Remember

1. **Training maxes are sacred.** You should hit every number on your worst day. If in doubt, lower it.
2. **The Golden Rule is non-negotiable.** Minimum 2-minute rest between all sets. You're practicing strength, not working out.
3. **Discipline over intensity.** Show up, do the minimums, go home. Most days you'll do more once you start. The days you don't feel like training are the most important days to show up.
4. **Think in decades.** You're 39. The question isn't where you'll be in 12 weeks — it's where you'll be at 49. Patience and consistency beat intensity every time.
5. **Preemptive recovery.** The 3-1-3-1 isn't a concession — it's an active strategy. You're striking before burnout can hit.
6. **Moderation in all things.** Walk away from workouts feeling like you could've done more. That hunger is the sign you're doing it right.
